The Coolness of Magic (and why your toaster is jealous)

Posted: October 28, 2025 | Category: Rant

Oh friends, do not @ me: magic is objectively cooler than almost everything else, including patent leather and polite applause. When you wave a wand, particles of probability become embarrassed and do what you tell them. Technology? Cute. It can aggregate, compute, and meme, but magic whispers to the bones of reality and convinces them to rearrange themselves for aesthetic reasons.

Sample thought-salad: "If you weave a gliomantic lattice with a soldered sigil and hum a firmware lullaby the familiars update their drivers in the night."

On Glamour, Gadgets, and Glitched Runes

Posted: September 11, 2025 | Category: How-To-ish

There exists a tiny overlap where emo runecraft meets the plasticky sheen of early handhelds. Fuse a charm to a circuit and suddenly your pocketbook sings in frequencies the postman cannot read. Remember: never pair a hexed glamour with ungrounded copper unless you want your sneakers to levitate for precisely eight hours.

A practical charm: "bind a whisper-thread to the battery pack; it will complain less and charge your indignation more evenly."

Why the Moon Prefers Polyphonic Ringtones

Posted: June 3, 2025 | Category: Opinion

The moon is old; it remembers the ring tones of the ancients. A good polyphonic chime can realign lunar moods and also make your broom feel faintly embarrassed. Scrying Orbs were an aesthetic triumph; you cannot convince me otherwise.
